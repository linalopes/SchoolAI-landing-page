01

Why this school
exists.

"AI is not a tool.
It is a material —
with grain, resistance,
and surprise."
— School of Tomorrow's AI

We live at an inflection point. Intelligent systems are reshaping how we create, learn, and relate to one another. Most education treats AI as software to be mastered. We treat it as a medium to be explored.

Room for Interaction is a series of workshops where the space itself becomes a playground for the body. Each session invites you to experiment with simple, creative interactions — voices that make paper flowers bloom, gestures that become sculptures, touches that light up space.

You don't need to code. You need curiosity.

Transformed Factory Space

A former factory now home to creative and research initiatives. Its industrial heritage provides the perfect backdrop for innovative workshops and artistic exploration.

Designed for Collaboration

With a dance studio, a prototyping workshop, and open areas for gatherings, the space is designed to support collaboration. The site itself becomes part of the experience.

05

The host.

Host
Lina Lopes

Artist, educator,
data scientist.

Lina Lopes is a Brazilian artist and creative technologist based in Zürich, Switzerland. With over a decade of experience across academic research, industry, and art, her practice weaves together interactive installation, machine learning, and embodied design.

She founded School of Tomorrow's AI as a space where technology becomes a tool for imagination — not a destination, but a material. A place where artists, designers, and curious humans can meet intelligent systems on their own terms.
